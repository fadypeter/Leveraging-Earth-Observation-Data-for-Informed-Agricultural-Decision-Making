Leveraging Earth Observation Data for Informed Agricultural Decision-Making

The integration of Earth Observation (EO) data with climate-smart agricultural practices represents a revolutionary step towards enhancing the resilience, efficiency, and sustainability of global farming systems. As climate change continues to intensify, the role of EO becomes indispensable in helping farmers adapt to new environmental realities while simultaneously improving productivity, resource use, and food security. Data Sources and Technologies EO data, collected from satellites, drones, and aerial platforms, offers a comprehensive and near-real-time perspective of agricultural landscapes. This data, captured through optical, radar, and thermal sensors, provides essential insights for informed decision-making in agriculture. Satellites like the European Space Agency’s Sentinel-2 and NASA’s MODIS, along with Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) systems such as Sentinel-1, offer a wide array of tools for crop monitoring, water resource management, and soil analysis.
